Shah Jahan, the fifth Mughal Emperor, ruled India from 1628 to 1658. He is renowned for his architectural achievements, particularly the Taj Mahal, a mausoleum built in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Shah Jahan's reign is often referred to as the "Golden Age" of Mughal Empire, marked by cultural, economic, and architectural prosperity. His passion for art and architecture led to the creation of numerous iconic monuments, showcasing his exquisite taste and grandeur, leaving a lasting legacy in Indian history. His love story with Mumtaz Mahal remains an eternal tale of devotion.
